# Module 1: Introduction to Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics

## Learning Goals

In this module, students will:
- Understand the foundational concepts of Physical AI
- Explore the design principles of humanoid robotics
- Identify key challenges and opportunities in the field
- Begin to think about practical applications of humanoid robots

## Week Overview

This week introduces the core concepts that will underpin the entire course. We'll examine what makes Physical AI distinct from traditional AI approaches, explore the rationale behind humanoid robot design, and consider the practical challenges involved in creating embodied intelligence.

## Required Readings

- Chapter 1: Introduction to Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics
- Supplementary reading on recent advances in humanoid robotics (TBD)

## Activities

1. **Reading**: Complete Chapter 1
2. **Discussion**: Participate in online forum about the future of humanoid robotics
3. **Exercise**: Research and report on one recent humanoid robot project
4. **Reflection**: Write a short essay on the potential impact of humanoid robots in society

## Assessment

- **Knowledge Check**: Online quiz covering key concepts from Chapter 1 (due by end of week)
- **Participation**: Active engagement in discussion forums
- **Assignment**: Research report on a recent humanoid robot project

## Prerequisites

There are no specific prerequisites for this module beyond general interest in robotics and AI.

## Estimated Duration

- Reading: 2-3 hours
- Activities: 3-4 hours
- Assessment: 2-3 hours
- Total: 7-10 hours for the week

Begin by reading Chapter 1, then participate in the discussion forum. The research assignment can be started early in the week to allow time for exploration of different humanoid robot projects.
